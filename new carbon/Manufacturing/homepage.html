Why Manufacturing Needs Emission Monitoring

Manufacturing accounts for nearly 30% of global CO₂ emissions, making it a critical sector for climate action. From heavy machinery to internal logistics and process heat, emissions like CO₂, nitrogen oxides (NOx), particulate matter (PM), and volatile organic compounds (VOCs) are generated at every production stage. These pollutants harm air quality, public health, and contribute to global warming.

In India, the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) mandates Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems (CEMS) for 17 highly polluting industries, including automotive, textiles, and food processing. Globally, companies like Tata Motors, Nestlé, Arvind Mills, Britannia, and Dixon Tech face pressure from regulators, investors, and consumers to reduce their carbon footprint. Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) reporting frameworks, such as GRI and BRSR, further demand transparent emission data to attract sustainability-focused investments.
