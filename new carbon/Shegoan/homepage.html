Call To Actions

Scope 1

Emission: Direct Emission

Scope 1 Emissions: Direct Emissions from Organizations Scope 1 emissions refer to direct greenhouse gas emissions produced by an organization from sources it owns or controls. These emissions result from activities such as manufacturing, fuel combustion, and company-owned transportation. Examples of Scope 1 Emissions: Manufacturing and Industrial Processes Industries like steel production, cement manufacturing, and chemical processing release emissions during production. These emissions come from burning fuels, chemical reactions, and other processes. Organization-Controlled Assets Company-owned vehicles such as trucks and cars that burn fuel. Boilers, furnaces, and generators used for energy production on-site. Refrigeration and air-conditioning systems that release greenhouse gases. Since these emissions are directly controlled by organizations, they play a crucial role in a company’s carbon footprint. Reducing Scope 1 emissions involves improving energy efficiency, using cleaner fuels, and adopting sustainable technologies.

Scope 2

Emission: Indirect Emission

Scope 2 (indirect) Indirect greenhouse gas emissions that arise from the generation of purchased electricity, heating, cooling, and steam. eg:- purchased electricity, steam, heating & cooling for own use

Scope 3

Emission: Indirect Emission

Scope 3(indirect) Other indirect greenhouse gas emissions that originate from activities relating to the value chain both upstream and downstream. These come from sources not owned or controlled by the organisation. eg:- purchased goods and services transportation & distribution waste generated in operation business travel employee commuting leased assets processing of sold products use of sold products fuel and energy related activities
